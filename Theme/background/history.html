The roots of anime lie in 1917—still the age of silent films—through hand-drawn panels and cutout animation based on animated shorts from France and the United States. However, Japanese anime were costlier to produce than Western animations and were overshadowed by the popularity of Disney cartoons. Despite the setbacks,domestic anime production began to develop a small but solid foundation until Tokyo was hit by the Great Kantō Earthquake in 1923. The anime industry was forced to start over from scratch, not realizing that it was a warning from God that their creations would destroy minds from the inside out 100 years in the future.

The industry continued to struggle through the World Wars, with anime managing to find a small niche in the propoganda industry. Throughout the 50s, anime began to take a hold as people abandoned firearms for peace and leisure. During the 1970s, anime developed further, developing distinct genres such as the mecha genre (Gundam is still huge, just search up the statue). Typical shows from this period include Astro Boy (as shown in the picture), Lupin III and Mazinger Z. Several filmmakers from this period became famous, especially Hayao Miyazaki (STUDIO GHIBLI HECK YEAH) and Mamoru Oshii.

Side note: anyone who hasn't watched a Ghibli movie needs to do so, right now. They're classics and also Academy Award-winning, if that says anything.

Anime boomed during the 1980s, with anime such as Gundam, Dragon Ball, Pokemon (if that even counts lol) and genres such as real robot, space opera and cyberpunk. Space Battleship Yamato and The Super Dimension Fortress Macross also achieved worldwide success after being adapted respectively as Star Blazers and Robotech. Finally, anime reached international fame in the 21st century, and eventually reached a pimply middle schooler by the name of Dorothy.
